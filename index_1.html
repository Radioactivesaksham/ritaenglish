IELTS

IELTS (International English Language Testing System) is the world’s most popular English language test. More than 2.9 million IELTS tests are taken each year.

Taking IELTS opens doors – it can help you live, study and work around the world. More than 10,000 organisations in 140 countries accept IELTS, including schools, universities, employers, immigration authorities and professional bodies.

IELTS assesses all of your English skills — reading, writing, listening and speaking, and is designed to reflect how you will use English at study, at work, and at play, in your new life abroad.

Marking Scheme for IELTS

Scores are given to candidates on band scale of 1-9 in each part of test. This part clearly depicts a candidate's ability to use and understand English language. IELTS results are valid for 2 years.

  • BAND 9-Excellent
  • BAND 8-Quite Good User
  • BAND 7-Good User
  • BAND 6-Fair User
  • BAND 5-Average User
  • BAND 4-Limited User
  • BAND 3-Very Very Limited User
  • BAND 2-Discontinuous User
  • BAND 1-Non User.
  • BAND 0-Practically No Knowledge of English Language.
